# Planning Process

The Group communication plan plays a central role as a point of reference and guidance. The divisional, functional, and regional team heads of internal and external communications collect inputs and document them on CPlan with a one-month-ahead focus.

## Long-Term Planning (Yearly)

- Starts in January with Group-wide relevance planning
- Captures an overview of internal, external, and social media key dates in the corporate calendar:
  - Quarterly results and AGM
  - Campaigns, conferences, and townhalls for all business divisions, functions, and regions
- Aligned with the year's objectives and key results (OKRs)
- Focus on avoiding overlaps and crowding
- In March, Head of Corporate Communications presents the long-term plan to the Group Executive Board
- Reviewed quarterly and updated if required

## Medium-Term Planning (Monthly)

- Handled on a monthly basis with weekly calls
- Planning lead + divisional, functional, and regional planning leads + editorial leads review the existing internal comms editorial planning process
- Focus narrows to one month ahead
- Editorial team defines priorities and approves proposals for specific channels (intranet, emails, townhalls) and categories
- Internal comms leads are empowered to take binding decisions

## Short-Term Planning (Daily)

- Planning lead manages ad-hoc deviations from medium-term planning
- Coordination with affected comms managers at Group, divisional, functional, and regional level on a daily basis
- In case of need for deviation and/or fast track: 15-minute stand-up calls
