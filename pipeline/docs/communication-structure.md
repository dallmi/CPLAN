# Communication Structure

Communications are organized in a three-level hierarchy: tracking clusters, communication packs, and communication activities.

## Hierarchy

```
Tracking Cluster          (top level — approved by ICPG)
  |
  +-- Communication Pack  (mid level — multi-channel, one objective)
        |
        +-- Activity      (bottom level — single item: email, event, article)
```

## Communication Activities

A communication activity is a single internal or external communication item:
- Email, article, event, e-screen, lockscreen, banner, townhall, etc.
- Can be part of a communication pack or stand-alone
- Regularly reviewed in planning sessions
- Both internal and external activities are tracked in CPlan
- Internal activities have two parts: the mandatory planning part and the pitch (for intranet articles only)

## Communication Packs

Communication packs group multiple activities around the same communication objective:
- Specified start and end date
- Multi-channel approach (email + intranet + event, etc.)
- Can include internal, external, or a mix of both activities
- Reviewed as part of strategic planning activities
- Can be linked to a tracking cluster or stand alone

Examples:
- Quarterly financial results
- Communication campaigns (e.g. "You're key", Craft, Global Learning Week)
- One-off events such as a launch of a new portal

## Tracking Clusters

Tracking clusters are the top-level grouping:
- Consist of at least two communication packs
- Used to analyze performance of communication packs in reference to similar activities
- Created and approved by the Internal Comms Planning and Governance team (ICPG) in coordination with analytics
- At the beginning of each year, ICPG agrees and creates new tracking clusters considering communication strategy and objectives

Examples:
- Financial reporting (with quarterly results as communication packs)
- Craft campaign (with waves/launch/activation as communication packs)
- Business Strategy (with quarterly updates as communication packs)

### Requesting a New Tracking Cluster

Additional tracking clusters can be requested by providing:
- Tracking cluster name (e.g. Financial reporting, Craft campaign, MyImpact)
- Rationale for creating the cluster (what data sets to compare)
- Cluster lead name (main contact and person responsible for data collection/analysis)
- Cluster lead team
- Strategic objective of communication in the cluster
- Estimated number of communication packs
- Senior leader(s) involved
